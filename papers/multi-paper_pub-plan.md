## 3. Self-Plagiarism Assessment

### 3.1 Estimated Textual Overlap

| Paper Pair | Overlap | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| arXiv vs IPR | ~60--65% | IPR is a substantial revision of arXiv. T&F and IPR both permit arXiv preprints. Acceptable: the preprint is a draft version, not a separate publication. |
| arXiv vs JCP | ~25--30% | JCP is a thorough reframing. Shared empirical base but different thesis, framework, and conclusions. Acceptable. |
| **IPR vs JCP** | **~15--20%** | **Most relevant comparison.** After all revisions, overlap is confined to factual descriptions of regulatory instruments. Analytical framing, theoretical apparatus, original contributions, discussion, and conclusions are entirely different. |

### 3.2 Verdict

**Both the IPR and JCP papers can be submitted independently without self-plagiarism.**

The academic standard for publishing from the same research programme across multiple venues requires that each paper makes a distinct analytical contribution to a distinct audience. This criterion is met:

1. **Different theses.** IPR argues for a multipolar regulatory landscape with European policy implications. JCP argues for a cyber governance trilemma with international security implications.
2. **Different theoretical apparatus.** JCP adds regime complexity, cyber sovereignty, and comparative regulatory capitalism grounding --- none present in IPR. The IPR paper has no cyber governance analysis.
3. **Different original contributions.** JCP contributes the trilemma concept, the regulatory timeline dataset, developing-nations cascade analysis, and the AI-cyber conflict subsection. IPR contributes the governance typology application and EU implementation analysis.
4. **Different audiences.** Internet policy scholars (IPR) vs cyber governance practitioners and security analysts (JCP). Minimal readership overlap.
5. **Different conclusions.** European policy recommendations (IPR) vs international cyber governance implications (JCP).
6. **Below overlap threshold.** At ~15--20%, textual overlap is below the informal ~25% threshold at which editors flag concern.

**Recommended disclosure:** Both the arXiv preprint and the IPR companion paper should be disclosed in the JCP cover letter (drafted at `papers/jcp2026/cover-letter.md`). The IPR submission need not disclose the JCP paper, but should note the arXiv preprint.

## 4. Submission Timeline

Both JCP and IPR operate on **rolling review** --- neither has fixed submission deadlines. This simplifies timing: the papers can be submitted concurrently.

### 4.1 Recommended Sequence

| Week | Action | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **Week 0** (now) | Send inquiry to JCP | Email Joyce Hakmeh (commissioning editor) with paper summary. Gauge editorial appetite before formal submission. Draft at `papers/jcp2026/email-hakmeh.md`. |
| **Week 0** | Submit IPR paper | IPR paper (`papers/ipr2026/`) is ready. Submit via ScholarOne or IPR's submission portal. Include note disclosing arXiv preprint. |
| **Week 1--2** | Await JCP editorial signal | If positive: proceed to full submission. If negative: pivot to alternative venues (see Section 5). |
| **Week 2--3** | Submit JCP paper | Submit full manuscript with cover letter disclosing arXiv preprint and IPR companion paper. |
| **Weeks 4--12** | Peer review (both journals) | JCP: review model not disclosed; expect 8--12 weeks. IPR: expect 6--10 weeks. |
| **Weeks 12--16** | Revise and resubmit | Address reviewer comments. The distinct framing of each paper means R&R requests will be independent. |
| **Weeks 16--24** | Acceptance/publication | Both journals have relatively fast post-acceptance production. |

### 4.2 Timing Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **IPR published before JCP review** | 30% | JCP reviewers see published IPR paper and flag overlap | Cover letter proactively discloses IPR; overlap at ~15--20% is below concern threshold |
| **JCP editor rejects on fit** | 15% | No JCP submission | Inquiry email (Week 0) tests fit before formal submission; pivot venues available |
| **Reviewer at both journals** | 5% | Same person reviews both papers | Extremely unlikely given different disciplines; distinct framing makes independent evaluation straightforward |
| **Both rejected** | 10% | Need alternative venues | Fallback venues identified (Section 5) |

### 4.3 Concurrent Submission Ethics

Neither JCP nor IPR prohibits concurrent submission of **different** papers to **different** journals. The ethical constraint is against submitting the **same** paper to multiple venues simultaneously. Since the two papers make distinct contributions (verified in Section 3), concurrent submission is permissible and disclosed in the JCP cover letter.
