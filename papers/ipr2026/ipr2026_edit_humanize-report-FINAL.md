### Edit 41 | H3.5 (mirror structure) | Discussion Comparative Synthesis

**Location:** Discussion (~line 372)

**Before:**
```
The EU occupies the centralised, ex ante quadrant: the AI Act establishes
an integrated, risk-based framework that regulates AI systems \textit{before}
they reach the market. The US sits in the distributed, ex post quadrant:
responsibilities are scattered across existing agencies, and enforcement
relies on existing tort, consumer-protection, and IP law. China occupies the
hybrid position: centralised guidance and formal registration requirements
coexist with decentralised, sector-specific enforcement and deliberate
forbearance for innovative SMEs.
```

**After:**
```
The EU sits squarely in the centralised, ex ante quadrant: the AI Act
establishes an integrated, risk-based framework that regulates AI systems
\textit{before} they reach the market. At the opposite corner, the US
distributes responsibilities across existing agencies and relies on tort,
consumer-protection, and IP law for enforcement. China falls between the
two, combining centralised guidance and formal registration requirements
with decentralised, sector-specific enforcement and deliberate forbearance
for innovative SMEs.
```

**Rationale:** Three consecutive "[Country] occupies/sits the X quadrant" sentences are the strongest mirror structure in the paper (H3.5). Each sentence now uses a different verb and opening: "sits squarely" / "At the opposite corner, distributes" / "falls between the two, combining." The spatial metaphors reinforce the typology quadrant imagery without mechanical repetition.
